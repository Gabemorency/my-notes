# Governing Documents

> [!note] About this document
> This is the operating constitution of The Commons Initiative. It captures how we make decisions, who has authority over what, and what we believe about our work. It is a living document — reviewed annually by the full board and updated when our understanding changes.
>
> Last reviewed: October 2024

---

## Our Values

These are not aspirational. They are operational — they describe how we actually make decisions when things are unclear.

**Institutional honesty.** We document what we know and what we don't. We record the reasoning behind decisions, not just the conclusions. When we were wrong, we say so.

**Continuity as infrastructure.** We build systems that survive transitions. Staff change. Leadership changes. The knowledge of the organization should not depend on any individual person's memory.

**Deliberate risk.** We take calculated risks when the expected value is clear and the downside is bounded. We document dissent. We revisit decisions when circumstances change.

**Community accountability.** Decisions that affect the communities we work with are made with, not just for, those communities. Our partnership agreements reflect this.

## Decision-Making Framework

Not all decisions are made the same way. This framework prevents both under-consultation and over-process.

### Tier 1 — Executive Decisions

Decisions the ED can make independently, within board-approved budget and strategy.

*Examples: hiring contractors, programmatic adjustments, vendor selection under $25,000, day-to-day operations.*

No required process. ED documents significant Tier 1 decisions in the decision log.

---

### Tier 2 — Consulted Decisions

Decisions the ED makes, but only after consulting relevant stakeholders.

*Examples: major partnership agreements, communications strategy shifts, programmatic pivots, any commitment over $25,000.*

> [!tip] Who to consult
> Consult depends on the domain: Finance decisions → Priya. Legal exposure → David. Community impact → Mireille (or equivalent partner). Board Chair is always available for a check-in when uncertain.

**Required process:** Document the consultation in the decision log, including who was consulted and what they said.

---

### Tier 3 — Board Decisions

Decisions that require full board vote.

*Examples: annual budget, executive compensation, new strategic initiatives, geographic expansion, major policy changes, ED hire or termination.*

> [!warning] Expansion decisions are Tier 3
> Any expansion into new geographies or program areas requires a board vote. The Eastside expansion (July 2024) was a Tier 3 decision — see [[ex-decisions#the-eastside-decision|the decision log entry]] for the reasoning and the 7–2 vote record.

**Required process:** 7-day notice to board, quorum of 5, simple majority. Dissenting votes are recorded and logged.

## Knowledge and Documentation Standards

> [!info] Why documentation is a governance issue
> Documentation is not an administrative burden. It is how an organization exercises accountability over time. A decision that can't be explained to a future staff member was probably not understood well enough when it was made.

Every significant decision — Tier 2 or Tier 3 — must be documented in the [[ex-decisions|decision log]] within two weeks of the decision being made. The entry must include:

- What was decided
- Who made or approved the decision
- What alternatives were considered
- Why this option was chosen
- What we expected to happen

Contact notes must be updated after every significant external conversation. The [[ex-contacts|contact manager]] is the institutional memory for relationships, not individual inboxes.

## Conflict of Interest Policy

Board members with conflicts on any item must recuse from discussion and vote on that item. Conflicts are defined broadly: financial interests, competing organizational affiliations, personal relationships that could impair independent judgment.

Fatima Al-Hassan (Communications) has a noted conflict due to employment at a competing organization. She recuses from any decisions related to that organization's program areas or funding relationships.

Conflicts must be disclosed at the start of any meeting where they are relevant, and noted in meeting minutes.

## Amendment Process

This document may be amended by a two-thirds board vote at any scheduled board meeting, provided the proposed amendment was circulated at least fourteen days in advance.

Amendments are tracked in version control. The history of this document is part of the record.
