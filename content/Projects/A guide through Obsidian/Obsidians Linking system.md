# Obsidian’s Linking System

Obsidian’s linking system is one of its core features, enabling you to create a dynamic, interconnected network of notes. Here's how it works:

### **1. Bi-Directional Links**

Obsidian allows you to create **bi-directional links** between notes. This means each linked note shows the connection, making it easy to navigate between related ideas. Just like how one thought leads to another, these links help you flow seamlessly through your knowledge base.

### **2. Easy Linking with Wikilinks**

Creating links is simple using **wikilinks** (e.g., `[[Note Title]]`). This ease of linking encourages you to connect ideas as they come up, rather than keeping them isolated. Over time, this builds a **web of knowledge**, where one note connects to many others.

### **3. Backlinks and Context**

Backlinks allow you to see **where a note is referenced** throughout your vault. This gives you instant context and highlights connections you might have missed. It’s like flipping through related sections in a book without losing track of your original thought.

### **4. Linking and Navigation**

Links in Obsidian aren’t just about connection—they enhance **navigation**. You can jump from one note to another, making your knowledge base easy to explore. This fluid structure lets you follow trains of thought and discover new relationships within your notes.
