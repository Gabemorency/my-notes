# Strongholds

---
>[!About]
This episode will talk about spiritual strongholds, what they are, how they are formed, and how to tear them down. 

```md
`Biblical Foundation`
2 Corinthians 10:3-6
For though we walk in the flesh, we do not war according to the flesh. For the weapons of our warfare are not carnal but mighty in God for pulling down strongholds, casting down arguments and every high thing that exalts itself against the knowledge of God, bringing every thought into captivity to the obedience of Christ, and being ready to punish all disobedience when your obedience is fulfilled.

```


## What are strongholds? 
- *Strongholds are thoughts and beliefs that the enemy will use to trap you in cycles and make it difficult for you to walk in the truth. Strongholds are areas in your life where it seems like the devil may reign supreme and you cannot escape sin or its effects.* 

>[!storytime!]
>Here, I will tell the story of when I struggled with lying and how I couldn't overcome it. And how as a result, I found myself wrestling against a stronghold that the devil had fortified over my life and that I couldn't seem to break out of. No matter how hard I tried, I couldn't seem to stop. Eventually however, what gave me freedom was realizing that the reason I lied so much was because I did not believe that the truth was enough and I ended up building an altar/idol out of my self image and sacrificing the truth at that altar. 
>When I realized this, I was able to tear down the idol I had built, and build an altar to God where I was able to sacrifice others opinions of me and live in the truth. 

### Strongholds are built through deception. 
- *Satans chief weapon against the life of a believer is deception, and he allows us to fall into strongholds through believing the lie.*
	- *Strongholds are not formed overnight though.*  Strongholds are  formed through habitual idolatry. 
	- The progression is usually gradual. 
		1. **Idol Elevation** – You begin to **elevate something above God** in your life. It might be success, relationships, money, or personal desires. Slowly, you start **neglecting your relationship with God** to nurture or pursue this thing.
		2. **Idolatry & Compromise** – What once seemed small now demands **sacrifices**—your time, integrity, and peace. You start **compromising your values** to maintain it, justifying behaviors that once convicted you.
		3. **Identity Connection** -  The idol **grafts itself to your identity**. It becomes a **source of worth, validation, or security**, making it harder to let go. You start thinking, _“Who am I without this?”_
		4. **Spiritual Bondage** – What was once a choice now **feels like a chain**. The idol controls your decisions, emotions, and outlook. Even when you see its destructive nature, you **struggle to break free** because it has **become a stronghold** in your mind and spirit.

### Strongholds manifest themselves in bad habits, addictions, and compulsive actions. 
- *Many people who are under a demonic stronghold might say things like...*
	- I want to stop, I just cant. 
	- I feel like I need this to get through the day or overcome this situation. 
	- I don't know why I do this anymore, I feel like I can't stop myself. 

### How Strongholds Control Behavior
- When a stronghold takes root, it operates like **a spiritual prison**, keeping a person bound to **self-destructive habits, addictions, and compulsions**.
	1. **It Begins with Justification** – _“It’s not that bad. I can stop whenever I want.”_
	2. **It Becomes a Dependency** – _“I need this to function, to cope, to feel happy.”_
	3. **It Turns into Bondage** – _“I don’t have control anymore. This controls me.”_


### Why are strongholds so dangerous? 
- Strongholds are so dangerous because they're not just about *habit formation*, they're also about *demonic possession and oppression.* 
	- Strongholds don't just trap you in sin, they open doors for the devil to gain access to you and your life. 
	1. **Strongholds Change Your Identity**
		- **Proverbs 23:7** – _“For as he thinks in his heart, so is he.”_
		- **Satan’s goal is not just to get you to sin—it’s to make sin your identity.**
		- A person struggling with lust, addiction, or fear eventually **stops seeing it as a problem and starts seeing it as part of who they are.**
		- This is why people say things like:
			- _“This is just who I am.”_
			- _“I can’t change. I was born this way.”_
			- _“I’ve been like this too long to be different.”_
		- **The longer a stronghold exists, the more it convinces you that change is impossible.**
	2. **Strongholds Invite Demonic Oppression & Possession**
		-  **Matthew 12:43-45** – _“When an impure spirit comes out of a person, it goes through arid places seeking rest and does not find it. Then it says, ‘I will return to the house I left.’ When it arrives, it finds the house unoccupied, swept clean and put in order. Then it goes and takes with it seven other spirits more wicked than itself, and they go in and live there.”_
		- **Strongholds create a dwelling place for demons.** If we allow a **habit, addiction, or sin pattern** to remain unchallenged, we are **creating a spiritual house for demonic influence**.
		- Many people struggle with **recurring sin cycles** because the enemy has taken **residence in that stronghold**.
		- ==When a stronghold is broken, it must be replaced with the presence of God—or the enemy will return even stronger.==
		- **The enemy isn’t just looking to tempt you—he’s looking to take territory in your life.**
	3. **Strongholds Affect Generations**
		-  **Exodus 20:5** – _“For I, the Lord your God, am a jealous God, punishing the children for the sin of the parents to the third and fourth generation of those who hate me.”_
		- Many strongholds are not just **personal struggles**—they are **generational patterns**.
		- **Examples of generational strongholds:**
			- Addiction
			- Poverty mindset
			- Anger and violence
			- Sexual immorality
			- Occult involvement
		- What one generation **tolerates**, the next generation **embraces**.
		- ==The sins you don't deal with will become the sins your children deal with.==
		- **Breaking a stronghold in your life doesn’t just set you free—it sets future generations free.**
	4. **Strongholds Distort Your Perception of God**
		-  **2 Corinthians 4:4** – _“The god of this age has blinded the minds of unbelievers, so that they cannot see the light of the gospel that displays the glory of Christ.”_
		- **A stronghold clouds your ability to see God clearly.**
		- A person with a **stronghold of fear** may struggle to trust God’s goodness.
		- A person with a **stronghold of rejection** may feel like God doesn’t love them.
		- A person with a **stronghold of addiction** may believe that holiness is unattainable.
		- **Satan builds strongholds to distort the way we see God, so we remain in bondage.**

## Breaking Free from Dangerous Strongholds

  >**2 Corinthians 10:4-5** – _“The weapons we fight with are not the weapons of the world. On the contrary, they have divine power to demolish strongholds.”_

  
1. **Recognize the Lie** – Identify the deception you have been believing.
	- You cannot tear down the stronghold without understanding the lie you are believing. This starts with knowing the truth. This is why knowing the word of God is so important. When we do not know the truth, many of us fall under spiritual strongholds and are completely unaware.  

2. **Renounce the Agreement** – Break the spiritual contract that gave the enemy access.
	- When we place our faith in Jesus Christ and stand on His Word, we enter into a covenant with Him, aligning our lives with His truth and authority. In the same way, when we place our faith in things, idols, or other people, we are entering into covenant with those things and ultimately, the enemy. 
		- Romans 6:16 - **Do you not know that to whom you present yourselves slaves to obey, you are that one’s slaves whom you obey, whether of sin _leading_ to death, or of obedience _leading_ to righteousness?**

3. **Replace with Truth** – Speak the Word of God over your mind and emotions.
	- It is not enough to simply stop the bad habit or break the negative cycle. You must *replace* it with truth based in the word of God. 
	- This means that you must remove the various triggers that my cause you to fall back into those negative habits. 
	- Speak truth over yourself and focus on the truth instead of the lie. 

4. **Rebuild a Godly Altar** – Establish a life of prayer, fasting, and obedience.
	- Prayer, fasting, Bible study and obedience to God are all things that make it very difficult for the enemy to gain a stronghold in your life.
