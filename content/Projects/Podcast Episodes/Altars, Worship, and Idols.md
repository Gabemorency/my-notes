# Altars, Worship, and Idols

---
>[!About]
This podcast episode will break down worship, altars and idols using the story of Abraham,  Gideon, and Cain and Abel as a biblical foundation. 

```md
`Biblical Foundation`
Gideon - Judges Chapter 6
Abraham - Genesis Chapter 22
Cain and Abel - Genesis 4
```

## What is an altar? 
- **An altar represents three primary things in the Bible.** 
	- *Firstly, whenever we see an altar, it represents a place where sacrifice happens.* Abraham sacrificed on an altar, Noah sacrificed on an altar, and in the old testament, we see the altars being used to make sacrifices on behalf of the sins of God's people. 
	- *Second, whenever we see an altar, it represents remembrance.* Altars were built as physical reminders of Gods presence, deliverance, or promise. Altars were used to remind Gods people of what He had done for them. 
	- *Lastly, Altars represent places of worship to God (that relates to sacrifice but we'll get there).* Altars were built to exalt the God they were built to and to honor and venerate that God. 

### Genesis chapter 22
- The story of Abraham sacrificing his son Isaac. 
	- Abraham told his servant that they were going to go *worship* even though he knew that he was going up the mountain to sacrifice his son. 
	- This signifies that worship is not what many of us think it is. It is not singing loud songs or jumping up and down to music. Worship is the act of sacrifice, and the altar determines the sacrifice that we make. 
	- When we look at this story, we often dismiss who exactly Isaac was to Abraham. Abraham was promised a son and waited *twenty five* for that son. He not only waited but he watched his wife age past the age of child bearing while still waiting for his promised son. This same son that he spent so much time waiting for, he was then ready to sacrifice. 

>[!main point]
True worship of God is sacrifice. Many of us think that we are worshiping God through singing songs or dancing, when in reality, we truly worship Him when we sacrifice our desire to sin and submit to His Lordship through obedience. Abraham could have said no to God. After all, Isaac was the son that God had promised Him. But he obeyed. Not even his own son was worth more to Him than God. I'm learning that anything that we are not willing to sacrifice to God is an idol. Anything that is more important to us than God is what is receiving our worship. We live in a society where we have somewhat normalized idolatry. It has become normal to say things like "my body my choice" or "my money my decision" or "my future, my life." But we don't realize that when we remove these things from the submission of God, we are actively placing them in the hands of Satan. 
It's also important to note that God was very specific with His request to Abraham. Often times, God asks us to make specific sacrifices and we *choose* to sacrifice what WE want to sacrifice, not what God wants. 

### Genesis 4
**We see this in the story of Cain and Abel**
- God asked both of them to prepare a sacrifice for Him, but the bible tells us that Abel prepared a sacrifice that pleased the Lord and Cain did not. 
- This tells us that what you sacrifice matters. 
- It's not enough for you to sacrifice what you want, you must sacrifice what God is asking for. This is true worship. 
---
Now if worship is sacrificing to God, then what is idolatry? 
>**Idolatry** is the act of giving **worship, devotion, or sacrifice** to anything or anyone other than God. It is the elevation of **a person, object, desire, ideology, or habit** to a place of ultimate importance, where it takes the position that belongs to God alone.**

A simple way to remember this is: What we are not willing to sacrifice is what we idolize. Anything that we are not willing to give up if God asked us to is an idol. For some of us, this involves relationships. For others of us, this involves money. For others of us, this involves success or glory. Anything that is more important to you than God is an idol. 

## Breakdown of altars and sacrifices

### Altar to God 
>This is the **only altar** that leads to life, transformation, and true fulfillment. When we sacrifice here, we surrender our will, desires, and fleshly nature in exchange for **God’s presence, guidance, and power**.

**Sacrifices made at Gods altar include**
- **Our Bodies** – _“Offer your bodies as a living sacrifice, holy and pleasing to God—this is your true and proper worship.”_ (Romans 12:1) → This means surrendering our actions, thoughts, and lifestyle to Him.
- **Our Plans & Desires** – Laying down our dreams, ambitions, and self-will to pursue God’s purpose.
- **Our Time & Resources** – Prioritizing prayer, worship, and service rather than selfish pursuits.
- **Our Comfort & Control** – Trusting God even when it means walking into the unknown.

### The Altar to Self **(Sacrifices of Pride & Autonomy)**
> This altar is rooted in self-worship, where we place **our desires, feelings, and independence above God**. The world often teaches that the highest goal is to **“be true to yourself”**, but when this turns into self-idolatry, we begin sacrificing things God never intended us to lose.

**Sacrifices We Make at the Altar of Self:**
- **Our Obedience** – Choosing our own way over God’s commands.
- **Our Relationships** – Damaging or losing relationships because of pride, selfishness, or an unwillingness to submit.
- **Our Spiritual Growth** – Avoiding spiritual disciplines because they require sacrifice or humility.
- **Our Peace** – Carrying burdens alone instead of surrendering them to God.

 **This altar leaves us empty and searching for something more.**


### The Altar of Success **(Sacrifices of Time, Family, & Integrity)**

>In a culture obsessed with achievement, many worship at the altar of **career, power, and influence**, sacrificing things that truly matter. While success isn’t inherently bad, when it **becomes our god**, we begin offering **dangerous sacrifices**.

**Sacrifices We Make at the Altar of Success:**
- **Our Time with God** – Prioritizing work, money, or social status over our spiritual lives.
- **Our Family & Relationships** – Neglecting loved ones in the pursuit of power or wealth.
- **Our Integrity** – Compromising values to get ahead.
- **Our Health & Rest** – Overworking and burning out for the sake of achievement.

  **This altar leaves us exhausted and unfulfilled, always chasing more but never feeling complete.**


### The altar of Pleasure **(Sacrifices of Purity & Conviction)**

>This is the altar where people sacrifice holiness and purpose for temporary gratification—whether through sexual sin, addiction, entertainment, or materialism. The world promotes **pleasure above purity**, leading many to offer sacrifices that leave them broken.

**Sacrifices We Make at the Altar of Pleasure:**
- **Our Purity** – Trading long-term fulfillment for short-term pleasure.
- **Our Convictions** – Lowering our moral standards to fit in.
- **Our Mental Clarity** – Overloading on entertainment, social media, or substances to numb emotions.
-  **Our Calling** – Letting distractions pull us away from God’s purpose.

 **This altar offers momentary pleasure but long-term emptiness.**


### The altar of Approval **(Sacrifices of Identity & Convictions)**

>Many people sacrifice their **true identity in Christ** at the altar of approval, living for validation from **friends, culture, or social media**. Fear of rejection leads them to compromise their beliefs or hide their faith.

**Sacrifices We Make at the Altar of Approval:**
-  **Our Authenticity** – Wearing a mask to fit in.
- **Our Boldness in Faith** – Being silent about Jesus to avoid conflict.
- **Our Convictions** – Doing what others expect instead of what God commands.
- **Our Peace** – Living in anxiety over what others think.

**This altar keeps us trapped in insecurity and fear.**

### Idolatry is the root of sin
> What we worship or idolize is what we allow to control us. So when we we are worshipping Christ, He is controlling us. However, when we are worshipping Idols, they control us. 

- Idolizing a relationship may be why simple mishaps make you so angry. 
- Idolizing money may be why missing out on an opportunity to make more money may make you so upset. 
- Idolizing others opinions of you may be why being disliked scares you so much. 

But even deeper than that, idolatry can lead you to act in ways that are completely uncharacteristic of yourself. Have you ever been so caught up in something, that eventually, you step back and realize that you don't even recognize yourself? Idolatry will do that to you. 
Idolatry can lead you to do terrible things in the name of what you worship. It can make you have sex to be accepted. It can make you steal, lie, and cheat to make money. It can make you mistreat people you love to look better in front of people you hate. 
### How Do We Tear Down the Wrong Altars?

>Just like Gideon in Judges 6:25-27, we must **tear down the altars that don’t honor God** and **rebuild an altar of true worship**.

-  **Repentance** – Acknowledge where we’ve been sacrificing at the wrong altar.

- **Realignment** – Recommit to sacrificing at God’s altar instead.

- **Renewal** – Replace unhealthy sacrifices with **daily worship, surrender, and obedience** to Christ. This is the most important step, as if you do not replace your idol worship with worship of God, you return back to the idol that you were delivered from. 

We all worship something...whether we want to admit it or not. 
Worship is not just action, it's direction. Whatever we worship, we are giving our devotion, trust, and sacrifice to something. The problem is that every altar outside of Christ demands sacrifices but gives nothing lasting in return. 

Making sacrifices at other altars may not seem bad at first...until the sacrifices start to cost you more than you wanted to give. Sacrificing time to be successful sounds good until you've made it, but you've neglected your family along the way. 
Sacrificing your identity to be accepted may feel good for a while until you find yourself alone and realize that you're not happy, and that even worse, you don't know what makes you happy. 
Sacrificing your body for pleasure may feel good until you eventually realize that that you're a slave to your sexual and physical impulses, and that you've developed a reputation that has damaged you. 

The thing with these other altars is that they all promise to give so much, but they always leave out what they take from you in return. 

Idolatry is not just a sin, it is the root of all sin. 

Lucifer worshipped himself above God, and as a result led the rebellion in heaven and was cast out. Adam and Eve worshipped pleasure and knowledge, and chose to eat the fruit because it was **”good for food and pleasing to the eye, and also desirable for gaining wisdom”** Even though God had given them express instructions not to eat it. 

When we obey Jesus, and truly worship Him through obedience, and surrender, we gain a unique kind of joy and peace that is difficult to describe. You become immovable, unshakeable, fearless, and bold. Because eventually, you come to realize that the only path to true freedom is to sacrifice self.

### Final point: Whenever God wanted to use someone, he required them to tear down their idols 

>Throughout Scripture, we see a consistent pattern: **before God could fully use someone, He required them to tear down their idols and destroy any false altars**. God demands **undivided worship**, and idols—whether physical, emotional, or spiritual—must be removed before a person can walk in their full calling.  

---

#### **1. Gideon: Tearing Down His Father’s Altar to Baal**  
📖 **Judges 6:25-26** – *“Tear down your father’s altar to Baal and cut down the Asherah pole beside it.”*  

 **Before God used Gideon to deliver Israel, He commanded him to destroy his father’s altar to Baal.**  
 **Lesson**: Before we can step into God’s power, we must remove any false sources of security, identity, or worship.  

---

#### **2. Abraham: Leaving Behind False Worship**  
📖 **Genesis 12:1** – *“Go from your country, your people, and your father’s household to the land I will show you.”*  

**Abraham was called to leave Ur, a place filled with idol worship, and follow the true God.**  
 **Lesson**: God often calls us away from familiar but ungodly environments before He can establish His promise in our lives.  

---

#### **3. Jacob: Removing Household Idols Before Encountering God**  
📖 **Genesis 35:2** – *“Get rid of the foreign gods you have with you, and purify yourselves.”*  

**Before meeting God at Bethel, Jacob commanded his household to bury their idols.**  
 **Lesson**: To have a fresh encounter with God, we must remove the things that compete for our devotion.  

---

#### **4. Israel: Destroying Idols Before Entering the Promised Land**  
📖 **Exodus 34:13-14** – *“Break down their altars, smash their sacred stones and cut down their Asherah poles. Do not worship any other god.”*  

**Before inheriting God’s promise, Israel had to tear down the altars of the Canaanites.**  
 **Lesson**: We cannot carry the baggage of idolatry into the new thing God wants to do in our lives.  

---

#### **5. The Rich Young Ruler: Called to Give Up His Wealth**  
📖 **Mark 10:21-22** – *“Go, sell everything you have and give to the poor, and you will have treasure in heaven. Then come, follow me.”*  

 **Jesus invited him to follow, but first, he had to let go of his idol—his wealth.**  
 **Lesson**: If something holds our heart more than God, He will ask us to surrender it before we can fully follow Him.  

---

####  The Principle: No Idols Before Calling  
👉 **God will not share His glory. Before He can fully use someone, they must tear down whatever competes with Him.**  
👉 **This isn’t just about physical idols—it includes pride, success, relationships, habits, or anything we worship above God.**
