# Decision Log

> [!note] What this is for
> This log captures major organizational decisions with their full context — what we knew at the time, what we considered, why we chose what we chose, and what we expected to happen. It exists so that future staff and leadership can understand not just *what* was decided, but *why* — and whether the reasoning held up.

---

## Decision: Adopt Markdown-Based Knowledge System {#knowledge-system}

**Date:** March 8, 2023
**Decision-maker(s):** Renata Osei (ED), Board approved at March meeting
**Status:** Implemented — 18 months in production

### Context

In early 2023, we completed our second leadership transition in eighteen months. The incoming Executive Director (Renata) spent her first six weeks reconstructing institutional context that should have been documented but wasn't — funding relationship history, program rationale, partner agreements, board decision records.

The estimated cost of that reconstruction: approximately 240 hours of staff time, plus several relationship conversations that should not have needed to happen.

### Options Considered

| Option | Pros | Cons |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Expand Notion usage | Familiar to staff, flexible | Expensive at scale, proprietary format, no version control |
| Confluence | Mature platform, good search | $600+/yr, built for engineering teams |
| Shared Google Drive + better naming conventions | Free, already in use | No relational structure, no version history, same problem |
| Markdown + Obsidian + Quartz + GitHub | Free, local-first, version-controlled, publishable | Learning curve, requires technical setup |

### Decision

Adopt the markdown-based system. Migrate key institutional documents over a 90-day period. Publish a restricted version of the knowledge base accessible to all staff and board members.

### Reasoning

The core argument: the problem isn't that we don't have tools. We have too many tools, and none of them are designed for what we actually need. A system that stores files in a format that will be readable in twenty years, that tracks every change, and that makes relationships between documents visible — that is qualitatively different from a better-organized Google Drive.

The cost argument: zero ongoing cost beyond a domain name. This removes the risk of a future budget decision eliminating the system.

The continuity argument: when the next transition happens, the new leader should be able to open a knowledge base and understand what we built and why. Not search through emails.

### What We Expected

- Initial resistance from staff accustomed to Notion (accurate — two staff members struggled in months 1–2)
- 90-day migration too aggressive (accurate — took 5 months for full adoption)
- Significant time savings on next leadership transition (to be measured)

### Outcome

Third leadership transition (October 2024) completed in two weeks. Previous transitions averaged four months for institutional context reconstruction.

## Decision: Eastside Expansion — Phased Approach {#the-eastside-decision}

**Date:** July 18, 2024
**Decision-maker(s):** Full board vote (7–2)
**Status:** Implementation in progress

### Context

Community demand data from our 2024 survey showed strong support for programming expansion into the Eastside corridor. Two board members opposed expansion due to financial risk — the Harlow Foundation renewal was not yet confirmed.

### Decision

Proceed with phased expansion. Phase 1 (two pilot sites) begins Q1 2025. Full rollout contingent on Harlow renewal confirmation.

### Reasoning

Delaying entirely means losing community momentum and potentially the Eastside Community Alliance partnership, which is time-sensitive. The phased approach limits exposure: if Harlow doesn't renew, we can absorb Phase 1 costs from reserves and pause before full rollout.

The two dissenting board members' concerns are noted and valid. This decision is a calculated risk, not a dismissal of the risk.

### What We Expected

Harlow renewal confirmation by October 2024. Pilot sites identified by November 2024.

### Current Status

Harlow confirmation pending (as of September 2024). Site assessment underway.
