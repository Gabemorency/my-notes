# What is worship?
###### Date: 2024-01-06

>[!Quote]
>"Worship is to acknowledge something as the authority in your life, to surrender to it"

The first time worship is mentioned in the Bible is in [[Genesis 22.3-5]]. Abraham is preparing to go *sacrifice* his only son because God told him to, and when preparing to take his son, Abraham tells his two other companions to wait where they are. He tells them that He is going to take Isaac, go and worship, and return. 
#### What worship is **Not**
In our modern culture, worship has evolved into this *activity* that involves outward expression and lacks inward transformation. We dance, we sing, we yell. This is not worship. As a matter fact, the Bible makes clear that God dislikes this style of worship. In [[Matthew 15.7-9]], when talking to the pharisees, Jesus Accuses them of practicing this style of worship actually calls this style of worship hypocritical! God is not impressed by our lavish songs or our fancy words. He is not moved by our theatrics and performances. Those things hold no weight in His eyes. Jesus' primary concern is our [[heart posture]].

#### Praise vs. Worship
What many of us consider worship is actually praise. Praise *is* an activity. Praise involves declaring God's goodness and expressing gratitude to God.

#### Worship in Greek and Hebrew
***Biblical Worship – Understanding the Greek and Hebrew***
Worship involves intimacy and submission, yielding to the Lord. Many of the Greek and Hebrew words used in the Bible show physical expressions and acts, like laying facedown, prostrated before the Lord. They show service and adoration. True worship is complete submission of ourselves before Him and involves more than verbal worship.

Songs alone are not worship. True Biblical worship involves a heart attitude and being yielded.

There is truth found in the words God has chosen to use to teach us about worship; however, it is possible to get overly technical with definitions and miss the spirit and heart of a subject. Get ahold of the spirit of what these words reveal to us. Take the time to study these words and consider how worship is demonstrated.

#### What is Praise?
Based on the greek and Hebrew definitions for praise in the Bible, we can see that praise IS in fact, an activity. Praise involves glorifying Jesus christ as our creator, singing songs to him, and expressing our gratitude to him in an outward physical way. Praise can involve singing, dancing, and any outward expression of thanks and gratitude to God. 

#### What is worship? 
Worship is VERY Different from praise. Praise is an activity. Worship is a lifestyle. Worship is an intimate state of being with God. True worship involves a heart that is totally submitted to God. As a matter fact, some of the words for worship in greek and Hebrew literally translate to being prostrate on the ground (an act of complete surrender in ancient times) and even to kiss, like how a dog licks his masters hand. Worship is all about the heart, and has little to do with your outward expression. This is where the pharisees got it wrong, and where many of us get it wrong today. We "worship" Jesus with hearts that are given over to [[idolatry]]. We sing songs to God and dedicate ceremonies to him but have no intention of obeying him or submitting to Him. Worship is when we die to ourselves, our desires, and our wills, and give the authority in our life, and the control over our attitude over to something or someone else. The essense of worship in its truest form is an act of complete and total surrender. 

#### Back to the story of Abraham
In the story of Abraham as mentioned above, Abraham described going up to the mountain to sacrifice his only son, an act of worship. And it was. Abraham was taking the thing most dear to him (his son) and giving him back to God through obedience. Abraham could have said "No God, this is the son you gave me, I will not sacrifice Him." But Abraham did not say that because *his heart was surrendered to God.* See, you cannot worship God while having other idols. And many of us have more idols than we think. God asked Abraham for his son intentionally. God knew that Isaac was what was most valuable to Abraham. There is a quote I love that says *"satan dines on whatever we withhold from the Lord."* True worship is essential to a deeper relationship with God because when we truly worship God, we are in essense, walking *in God*. When we worship, we are completely given to the holy spirit and we are allowing Him to use us as instruments for Him. I believe that many people have gotten very good at praise, but there are oh so few, who truly worship God.

#### What does it mean to truly worship then?
To truly worship then, is to take all our wants, all our desires, all our hopes, goals and dreams, look to God and say *"Here. Everything that I want, everything that I hope to have or be, it's all yours. I know that you want the best for me and will withhold no good thing from me, and out of love and devotion to you, I will withhold nothing from you either.* When we are worshipping God, we are telling God that He alone, is enough, that He is all we need. To worship God is to have no idols, and to desire closeness, obedience, and intimacy with God above all else. You cannot desire something more than God and surrender to Him at the same time. To worship God means that you have let go of everything, and have decided to pursue holiness through surrender and devotion to God. [[Matthew 6.33]] says: <sup>33</sup> But seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all these things shall be added to you. When you surrender to God, you trust that If you seek God, He will give you everything you need, AND more. You don't seek out the thing. You become the person that God is calling you to be, and as you grow in Christ, God adds unto you. Worship prepares us for communion with God. There is no prayer, there is no faith, there is no kingdom work without worship of God. God **demands** our worship. When we worship God, we are preparing ourselves to do His will through us. It is impossible to walk in the will of God, to walk hand in hand with God, to draw close and hear the tickle of his ever illusive whisper without a heart of worship bent towards Him. 

#### Why do we worship?
When we worship, we are fixing our eyes on God. When we worship, we are reminding ourselves that [[God is in total control]]. Worship serves as a declaration to God: that in the midst of all that is happening, we still choose to trust in Him. 
Worship teaches us, worship serves as fellowship between us and God, and worship serves as our gift to the Lord. 
- **Worship Teaches us**
	- Worship teaches us to submit and surrender all our cares, hopes, dreams, problems, and aspirations to God. Worship teaches us how to have faith. Faith at its core, is lived-out worship. Worship reminds us of our place and it remind us of God's. 
- **Worship serves as fellowship between us and God.**
	- When we worship, we are communing with God. When we are fully surrendered to His will, we are then able to receive His word. Many times, the reason we cannot hear God's voice is because our voice is exceptionally loud while God speaks in a whisper. Hearing God's voice is often a matter of minimizing our own voices, and maximizing God's. That in and of itself is an act of surrender because by minimizing your voice and maximizing His, you're saying: *"Father, I know that you know what it is that I want. But I will put your will above my desires and trust that you will lead me to what it is my heart truly desires. I know what I think I want but I am trusting you to give me what I truly want and need.*
- **Worship is our gift to the Lord**
	- God is good, and He, as the king of the universe, deserves our worship. We worship God because he is the king of the universe, our creator, awesome and mighty, deserving of all of our worship and praise. He is amazing and almighty, He is matchless and magnificent, He is a king, and He is a conqueror, He is the beginning and the end, He is my savior, yet He is also a servant, He is my father, and He is my foundation, He is the author and He is the answer, He is good, and He is GOD.

#### The conclusion...for now
Anything that we withhold from God, we are by nature, giving to the devil. God is in heaven, but through His spirit, he whispers to us. *"my Child, draw near to me. Come and change the world with me.* He invites us to lay off our flesh and put on righteousness. He promises the adventure of a lifetime, if only we would leave it all behind to follow Him. To take the hand of God, to let go of what you thought your life was supposed to be, and embark upon a life of biblical proportions is the invitation that is offered to each of us. Will you accept? Will you throw yourself down upon the feet of the throne of grace, in awe of God's power and righteousness, willing to abandon all to follow Him? Would you give it all up if He asked you to? God longs to raise a generation of individuals who seek His face, and at the slightest whisper of His word would attempt the impossible because He says it. Are you surrendered to God? What are you with holding from Him? What are you afraid to lose? Because when you surrender to God, when you worship Him, you gain everything. What you give to God, He always gives back, but better. **God is waiting for you.** He stands at the door, and knocks asking: *"are you ready to give it up? Are you ready to follow me."* **SAY YES!!!** Nothing on this earth can compare to being emptied of the world and being filled with God!! He will plant you like a tree, immovable, unstoppable. The World will look at you and marvel, *"What does He have? What does she have? Where is his/her fear? Where is his/her anxiety? Where is his/her limit?* And your response will be: "I gave up everything I had, but I found everything I needed in the Lord." **Be a living witness!! Demonstrate faith like Abraham. Courage like David. Wisdom like Solomon. Authority like Elijah. Peace like Paul. Love like Jesus. Child of God, live like Jesus died for you!** Surrender it all to God. Forget the loud music, the songs, the dancing and the noise. Find your secret place. And painstakingly give up everything to the Lord. Worship Him. Exalt Him. And through you, He will accomplish wonders.
