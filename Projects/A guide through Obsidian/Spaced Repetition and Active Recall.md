## Spaced Repetition & Active Recall

Obsidian can significantly enhance memory retention when you combine **spaced repetition** and **active recall** with its powerful linking system. Here's how:

###### **Spaced Repetition**
- **Definition**: Spaced repetition is the practice of reviewing information at increasing intervals to optimize long-term retention.
- **How it works in Obsidian**:
  - Use links to **revisit key notes**. When you link to related concepts, you can easily re-engage with your notes.
  - Set a **reminder system** within Obsidian (either manually or with a plugin) to review notes at specific intervals.
  - This method ensures that you are **spacing out review sessions**, helping reinforce the connections between ideas before they fade from memory.

###### **Active Recall**
- **Definition**: Active recall involves retrieving information from memory without relying on the original source, improving memory strength and recall speed.
- **How it works in Obsidian**:
  - Create **question-based links**. For example, turn each note into a prompt (e.g., “What are the key ideas from this note?”).
  - As you click through linked notes, mentally recall the details you learned earlier, engaging in active recall as you navigate your knowledge base.
  - By **retesting yourself** using your notes and their links, you're engaging in active recall throughout your workflow.


###### **combined Results**
- **Reinforces Connections**: As you combine spaced repetition with active recall, you're not only reviewing at the right intervals but also reinforcing the **connections between related notes**.
- **Improves Retention & Understanding**: The consistent review of concepts, paired with active engagement, boosts **long-term retention** and **deeper understanding** of your material.
- **Promotes Efficient Learning**: You can cover more material in less time, since you’re actively reviewing and reinforcing notes that require attention, while allowing your brain to forget and relearn over time.

By leveraging these strategies, you’ll retain more information, recall it faster, and understand it on a deeper level.
