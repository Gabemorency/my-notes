# How Obsidian mirrors the brain 
## The Brain as a Network

Obsidian’s design mirrors the way the brain processes, organizes, and connects information. Here’s how:
### **1. Neural Connections**

Just like neurons in the brain form **connections** to create memories and ideas, Obsidian allows you to link notes together. These **bi-directional links** mimic how the brain associates one thought with another, enabling a web of interconnected knowledge.

### **2. Association and Recall**

The brain retrieves memories by triggering **associations**. Similarly, Obsidian allows you to create links between ideas, so when you revisit a note, you’re reminded of related concepts. This enhances **contextual recall**, helping you make connections more easily.

### **3. Non-Linear Thinking**

The brain doesn’t process information in a straight line—it uses a **non-linear approach**, jumping from one thought to another. Obsidian replicates this by allowing you to organize and link notes without rigid structures, facilitating more **creative thinking** and exploration of ideas.

### **4. Dynamic Growth**

The brain continuously forms **new pathways** as we learn and adapt. Similarly, Obsidian is flexible, allowing you to **add, change, and expand** your knowledge base. As your thoughts evolve, Obsidian evolves with you, supporting lifelong learning.

---

In short, Obsidian mirrors the brain by allowing you to create a **connected, evolving web of ideas**, reflecting the brain’s dynamic, associative, and non-linear processing of information.
